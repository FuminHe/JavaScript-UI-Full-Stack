Difference between CSS Psudo Elements and Psudo Classes.

1.A pseudo-class is a selector that selects elements that are in a specific state, e.g. they are the first element of their type, or they are being hovered over by the mouse pointer. They tend to act as if you had applied a class to some part of your document, often helping you cut down on excess classes in your markup, and giving you more flexible, maintainable code.
2.Pseudo-elements behave in a similar way, however they act as if you had added a whole new HTML element into the markup , rather than applying a class to existing elements. Pseudo-elements start with a double colon ::.
